## 1. Method

### 1.1 The Analytical Frame

The MTG Knowledge Complex (mtg-kc) defines 25 color signatures organized as a simplicial complex over the five Magic: The Gathering colors (White, Blue, Black, Red, Green). The simplices decompose into three layers:

- **5 vertices** (0-simplices): single colors, each embodying a pure philosophical orientation
- **10 edges** (1-simplices): color pairs, each capturing a generative tension or synergy between two orientations
- **10 faces** (2-simplices): color triples, each expressing a three-way dynamic with emergent properties not reducible to its constituent pairs

### 1.2 The Three-Tier Evidence Model

Evidence used in this analysis is classified by discoverability and legibility:

| Tier | Label | Description | Examples in this case |
|------|-------|-------------|----------------------|
| 1 | Private / Internal | Motivations, values, felt sense. Requires subject self-report or guided introspection. Self-identification is evidence, not proof. | Feedback session; Temur self-ID; statements about private working orientation |
| 2 | Low-discoverability, low-legibility public | Publicly accessible but requires prior context to locate. Fills gaps; checks claims. | Ultimate frisbee interview; PADA.org board history; Human Spectrum art participation; LAMP DAO; Mutualist Society involvement |
| 3 | High-discoverability, high-legibility public | Standard web-findable. Reliable for public figures with authentic personas, but persona ≠ intrinsic self. | Academic papers; BlockScience blog; GitHub; organizational affiliations; LinkedIn; Twitter |

The original v1 analysis relied almost entirely on Tier 3 evidence. This revision treats all three tiers as inputs, weighted by their proximity to the underlying phenomena (motivation, identity, practice) rather than their abundance.

### 1.3 Color Observability Fingerprints

A critical methodological insight: different color orientations produce systematically different amounts and types of observable evidence. An analysis that ignores this will have skewed confidence calibration — high confidence where evidence is abundant (Blue-heavy territory) and spuriously low scores where evidence is structurally rare (Red-heavy territory).

| Color | Tier 3 Legibility | Why | Bias Risk |
|-------|------------------|-----|-----------|
| **Blue (U)** | Very high | Blue *is* documentation: papers, frameworks, code, formal specifications. Every Blue behavior generates a paper trail. | Overcounting — abundance of Blue evidence may reflect the nature of documentation, not the depth of the orientation |
| **White (W)** | High | Institutions are legible: organizational affiliations, governance structures, charters, public positions, professional ethics statements | Moderate — visible in formal shell; the *felt obligation* behind it is Tier 1 |
| **Green (G)** | Medium | Community memberships and organizational affiliations show up; but Green's deepest orientation (felt ecological acceptance, organic humility) is internal | Moderate — affiliations visible, inner stance requires Tier 1 |
| **Red (R)** | Low | Passion, spontaneity, embodied action, ephemeral experience don't leave clean records. Sports, art, hacker events are precisely the low-legibility activities. | Systematic undercounting — any analysis relying on Tier 3 will underestimate Red |
| **Black (B)** | Very low (by design) | Strategic self-interest is not publicly broadcast. Cannot distinguish "Black hiding" from "Black absent" without Tier 1. | Ambiguous — low score could mean genuinely low Black, or Black is present and private |

**Implication for this case:** v1 of this analysis was reliable for Blue and White, reasonably reliable for Green at the organizational level, systematically underpowered for Red, and unable to adjudicate Black. This revision applies an explicit upward correction to Red where Tier 1/2 evidence confirms its presence, and maintains acknowledged uncertainty about Black.

This is not aspiration vs. reality. It is **two real modes of being with different observability profiles**. The subject is both a Bant practitioner (in his public institutional role) and a Temur practitioner (in his embodied and experimental life). The original analysis could only see one mode.

## 6. Summary of Findings (Revised)

The revised analysis confirms the v1 core finding — Blue-Green (Simic) is the central axis — while substantially revising the role of Red and the accuracy of the Temur self-identification.

**What the observability analysis changes:**

1. **Red is real and was systematically undercounted.** The 2.5 score in v1 was an artifact of analyzing a low-Tier-3-legibility color from Tier 3 evidence. The revised score of 4.5 is almost certainly still an undercount — there is likely more Tier 2 Red evidence that would be surfaced by fuller investigation.

2. **The Temur self-ID is accurate.** The v1 conclusion ("Temur is aspirational") was wrong. Temur describes a real mode of practice that was simply invisible in the Tier 3 record. Self-identification, when it diverges toward lower-legibility colors, deserves suspension of skepticism pending Tier 2 investigation.

3. **Bant is the public face of Temur, not a competing identity.** The institutional and intellectual outputs (Bant) are how Temur-energy takes durable form. They are not separate. Blue-White-Green is what you see in the record; Blue-Green-Red is what generates the energy that creates the record.

4. **The methodology matters.** This case study is a demonstration that the tier of evidence shapes the portrait you get. An exclusively Tier 3 analysis would conclude Bant. A fuller analysis concludes Temur expressed through Bant. The difference is meaningful and would affect any application of the analysis (hiring, collaboration, pedagogy, mentorship).

**Residual uncertainty:**

- **Black:** Tier 3 is reliably low; Tier 1 does not add positive Black evidence; but the inherent privacy of Black means this can't be fully resolved without deeper Tier 1 inquiry.
- **Red (upper bound):** The 4.5 score is probably still an undercount. The evidence base for Red is improving but not complete. The true score likely lies in the 4.5–6.0 range.
- **Persona vs. intrinsic:** This analysis has treated the Tier 3 record as a reliable (if incomplete) proxy for intrinsic orientation, supplemented by Tier 1/2. This is reasonable given the subject's stated authenticity — but the theoretical question remains open as a general methodological matter.
